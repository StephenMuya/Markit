# Product Strategy: Markit (Texas + New York)

## Product Definition
Markit is a location-aware real estate intelligence platform that transforms CRE media and market sources into structured, monetizable local signals.

## Strategic Objective
Build a repeatable intelligence product that reaches revenue quickly through subscriptions, beginning with Texas and New York.

## Customer Segments and Jobs-to-Be-Done

### 1) Agents
- Identify neighborhood-level talking points before competitors
- Advise clients on upcoming supply and change drivers
- Stay informed without reading every source manually

### 2) Brokers
- Track active owners, developers, lenders, and deal momentum
- Build client-facing intelligence views by geography
- Detect category movement (office, multifamily, mixed-use, retail)

### 3) Investors
- Source opportunities and monitor market timing indicators
- Follow debt/liquidity conditions and distress patterns
- Compare market heat across TX and NY submarkets

### 4) Homebuyers
- Understand neighborhood trajectory and risk signals
- Monitor nearby construction and pipeline pressure
- Access plain-language local briefings

## Premium Signal Families
1. Development Pipeline
2. Deal Activity
3. Financing Activity
4. Distress Indicators
5. Neighborhood Change

## Product Principles
- **Location-first:** every insight is geo-aware and filterable
- **Signal-first:** structured events over raw article dumps
- **Action-first:** alerts, watchlists, and digest outputs
- **Monetization-first:** every major feature ties to willingness-to-pay

## Initial Geographic Scope
- Texas: Austin, DFW, Houston, San Antonio
- New York: NYC boroughs first, then broader NY market coverage

## Success Metrics
- Paid conversion rate by persona
- Weekly active users on local feeds/alerts
- Alert open/click-to-action rate
- 30/60/90-day subscription retention
- Revenue per active geography
